So the Apple event came and went. Let's compare go over last month's predictions.
<hr />
<ul>
<li><span style="color:#696969"><strong>So what will we learn from the September 10th event?</strong></span></li>
<li><span style="color:#696969">How will it affect businesses? </span></li>
<li><span style="color:#696969">How will it affect developers? </span></li>
</ul>
<span style="color:#696969"> ...Honestly probably not much. <strong>Since the iPhone X, we've seen little changes when it comes to the iPhone.</strong><br />
...</span><br />
<a href="https://stratechery.com/2019/apples-audacity/"><span style="color:#696969"><strong>The iPhone, while it's most successful product, has plateaued</strong>.</span></a><span style="color:#696969"> The closest preview of this was this year's WWDC Keynote.</span>
<hr />
<span style="color:#B22222">The iPhone has plateaued but I think it's interesting they branded the over $1,000 phone has the pro model and emphasized photography. It's a smart move.<br />
Also this may be the right time to think about having an app since <strong>most iPhones folks use now are fairly mature and stable. I don't think there will radical changes to the iPhone in the 2-3 years.</strong></span>
<hr />
This year we see rumors regarding:
<ul>
<li>a third lens for better photos ✅</li>
<li>perhaps a new Apple Watch ✅</li>
<li>maybe new AirPods?? ❌</li>
</ul>
<hr />
<span style="color:#B22222">2/3... Not Bad 👍</span>
<hr />
Apple is more focused in general on:
<ul>
<li>Wearables (Apple Watch &amp; AirPods)</li>
<li><em>Pro Hardware like the </em>iPad and the Mac</li>
<li>Machine Learning and Augmented Reality</li>
<li><em>"Services"</em> (Video Streaming, Music, Games, App Store, etc...)</li>
</ul>
<hr />
<span style="color:#B22222">I think this still holds true. T<strong>he iPhone is still the big cash cow for Apple but growth is going to be in those markets and technologies. </strong></span>
