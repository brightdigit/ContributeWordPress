<td style="text-align: left;" class="mcnTextContent" data-valign="top" style="padding-top: 0; padding-right: 18px; padding-bottom: 9px; padding-left: 18px; mso-line-height-rule: exactly; -ms-text-size-adjust: 100%; -webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%; word-break: break-word; color: #202020; font-family: Helvetica; font-size: 16px; line-height: 150%"><strong>Having worked remotely for almost 10 years,</strong> I can attest that it can be a challenge for many workers and companies. So here are a few quick tips:
<ul>
<li><strong>Stay Connected but Stay Asynchronous</strong><br />
Asynchronous means that when you send a message out - don't expect a reply right away. While many managers demand quick replies, we know interrupting people as they work can be very detrimental to their productivity</li>
<li><strong>Have a Specific Place and Time for Work</strong><br />
There are two problems when you don't have a specific place and time. It allows you to be distracted but it can also result in you not having boundaries. Without those boundaries, you can find yourself working anytime and all the time. In the end, that can hurt your productivity but also your health.</li>
<li><strong>Have <em>Water Cooler</em> Breaks</strong><br />
At an office, we find ourselves talking to fellow employees at the break room or at their cubicle. Make sure to do the same at home. Take 15 minute break every 2 - 3 hours: grab a cup of coffee, go on a short walk, watch a quick YouTube video, etc...</li>
</ul>
If you are interested in a great set of apps to help you and your team work remotely, <a href="https://leogdion.name/2019/08/01/productivity-apps-for-developers-and-everyone/">check out my blog post on productivity apps.</a> Most importantly be easy on yourself and make sure to follow the right guidelines to try and stay healthy.</td>
